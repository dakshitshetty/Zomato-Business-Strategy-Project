# Zomato — March 2026 Business Strategy Project

## Overview
This project studies the plateauing growth in Zomato’s core food delivery business and evaluates whether aggressively scaling Blinkit, its quick commerce arm, is strategically and financially viable in a highly competitive market.

## Problem Statement
Zomato’s food delivery user growth has started to slow down in mature markets. At the same time, Blinkit has strong growth potential, but it also comes with higher operational complexity, intense competition, and heavy dependence on dark store efficiency.

The project focuses on two main questions:
- How can Zomato improve user growth in its core food delivery business?
- Is aggressive Blinkit expansion profitable and sustainable in the long run?

## Approach
To analyze the problem, I used a mix of strategic and quantitative methods:

- **Porter’s Five Forces** to evaluate competition in food delivery and quick commerce.
- **Churn analysis** to understand user retention patterns.
- **Cohort analysis** to track customer behavior over time.
- **CAC vs LTV analysis** to assess customer acquisition efficiency.
- **Contribution margin analysis** to measure profitability per order.
- **Break-even analysis** to estimate the viability of dark stores.
- **Operational efficiency review** of the Blinkit dark store network.

## Key Insights
- Food delivery growth is slowing due to market maturity and rising competition.
- Blinkit has strong strategic potential, but profitability depends heavily on scale and operational discipline.
- Dark store density plays a major role in controlling last-mile delivery costs.
- Users who engage with both food delivery and Blinkit show stronger retention and higher lifetime value.
- A unified loyalty program can improve cross-selling and reduce churn across both services.

## Recommendations
- Optimize dark store density to reduce last-mile delivery costs.
- Improve inventory and demand forecasting using predictive models.
- Introduce a tiered loyalty program across food delivery and Blinkit.
- Use route optimization algorithms to reduce delivery time and operational cost.
- Focus Blinkit expansion only in markets where unit economics are strong enough to support scale.

## Technical Methods
- Predictive machine learning models for demand forecasting
- Route optimization algorithms for delivery efficiency
- Cohort-based retention tracking
- Break-even modeling for dark store profitability
- Scenario analysis for expansion planning

## Conclusion
The project suggests that Zomato should not scale Blinkit blindly. Instead, it should expand selectively, improve operational efficiency, and build a stronger ecosystem between food delivery and quick commerce through better retention and cross-selling strategies.
